Fields of inquiry.

The questions I keep returning to — about the universe, intelligence, consciousness, and the technologies that may define what comes next. Essays in these territories are gathered here.

i.Cosmology

The Universe & Cosmology

I enjoy exploring questions about the origin, structure, and ultimate fate of the universe. Topics such as black holes, dark matter, dark energy, the Big Bang, extraterrestrial civilisations, and the possibility of a multiverse continue to shape how I think about existence and our place within it.

ii.Physics

Physics & Fundamental Reality

Physics represents humanity's attempt to understand the deepest laws governing reality. I am particularly interested in quantum mechanics, the nature of time, entropy, information theory, and the search for a unified framework that explains the universe at its most fundamental level.

iii.Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence & The Future of Intelligence

The evolution of intelligence, from biological systems to artificial ones, is one of the most transformative developments in human history. I write about AI agents, AGI, autonomous systems, alignment, and how intelligent machines may reshape science, work, and civilisation in the decades ahead.

iv.Mind

Consciousness & The Mind

Consciousness remains one of the greatest unsolved mysteries in science. I enjoy exploring questions surrounding awareness, subjective experience, free will, intelligence, and whether consciousness can emerge from sufficiently complex computational systems.

v.Civilisation

Technology & Civilisation

Technology has always been the engine of human progress. My interests include the long-term evolution of computing, human augmentation, robotics, space exploration, and the technologies that may define future civilisations.

vi.Synthesis

Where These Ideas Meet

The ideas that fascinate me most often exist at the intersection of physics, intelligence, computation, and consciousness. Understanding how these domains connect may ultimately help us better understand both ourselves and the universe we inhabit.
