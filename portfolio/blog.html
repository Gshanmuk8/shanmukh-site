Collected works · Ch. III — Fields of inquiry

Fields of inquiry.

The questions I keep coming back to — about the universe, intelligence, consciousness, and the tech that might shape what comes next. I've gathered essays on these topics here.

i.Cosmology

The Universe & Cosmology

I really enjoy digging into questions about where the universe came from, how it's put together, and how it might eventually end. Stuff like black holes, dark matter, dark energy, the Big Bang, alien civilisations, and the whole idea of a multiverse keeps shaping how I think about existence and our place in it.

ii.Physics

Physics & Fundamental Reality

Physics is basically humanity trying to understand the deepest laws behind reality. I'm especially interested in quantum mechanics, the nature of time, entropy, information theory, and the search for one unified framework that explains the universe at the most basic level.

iii.Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence & The Future of Intelligence

The way intelligence has evolved, from biological systems to artificial ones, is one of the biggest changes in human history. I write about AI agents, AGI, autonomous systems, alignment, and how intelligent machines might reshape science, work, and society in the coming decades.

iv.Mind

Consciousness & The Mind

Consciousness is still one of the biggest unsolved mysteries in science. I like exploring questions about awareness, subjective experience, free will, intelligence, and whether consciousness can come out of complex enough computational systems.

v.Civilisation

Technology & Civilisation

Technology has always been what drives human progress forward. I'm interested in the long-term future of computing, human augmentation, robotics, space exploration, and the technologies that might define future civilisations.

vi.Synthesis

Where These Ideas Meet

The ideas that fascinate me the most usually sit where physics, intelligence, computation, and consciousness all overlap. Understanding how these areas connect might eventually help us understand both ourselves and the universe we live in.
