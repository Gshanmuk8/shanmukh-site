No. 01

FitAI

AI-Powered Fitness Coach & Progress Tracker

Stack — JavaScript · React · Vite · Node.js · Express.js · Supabase · Google Gemini API

  • Identified a common problem of poor accountability and inconsistent progress tracking in personal fitness journeys.
  • Designed and built a full-stack, AI-powered fitness platform enabling users to log workouts, monitor progress, and receive personalised coaching guidance.
  • Implemented secure authentication, real-time data management, and AI-driven insights using the Google Gemini API.
  • Architected a multi-provider AI cascade with health monitoring and automated fallback logic — if Gemini is unavailable the system falls back to OpenRouter, and then to Groq — ensuring reliable AI responses within free-tier limitations.
  • Took the product from initial idea and problem discovery to deployment and real-world usage, learning how products evolve through user feedback.
  • Focused on building features that improve consistency, motivation, and accountability rather than simply tracking workout data.
  • Continuously iterated on the platform based on testing and usage patterns to understand what actually delivers value to users.
  • Gained first-hand experience balancing engineering decisions, user experience, and product priorities while shipping an AI-driven application.
Field note

The hardest problem wasn't the AI — it was reliability. A coach that sometimes doesn't answer is worse than no coach, which is why the provider cascade exists.

No. 02

SkillMesh

Community-Driven Skill-Sharing Platform

Stack — TypeScript · React · Vite · Node.js · Express.js · Supabase

  • Identified an opportunity for a platform centred on practical, peer-to-peer skill exchange rather than passive content sharing.
  • Designed and built a prototype enabling users to discover others by skill, showcase what they can teach, and connect to learn.
  • Applied product thinking by validating the concept through rapid prototyping and iterative feature development.
  • Explored mechanisms for matching users based on complementary skills, interests, and learning goals to encourage meaningful connections.
  • Focused on reducing friction in discovering mentors, collaborators, and peers within local and online communities.
  • Designed the platform around active participation and knowledge exchange rather than passive content consumption.
  • Used rapid iteration and feature experimentation to understand which interactions created genuine value for users.
  • Strengthened my understanding of community-driven products, user engagement, and the challenges of building network-based platforms.
Field note

Network products live or die on their first interactions. Most of the iteration went into the moment two people with complementary skills actually find each other.

No. 03

LocalLens

Zero-Friction Local Discovery Platform

Stack — React · Vite · OpenStreetMap · Leaflet.js · Nominatim API · Overpass API

  • Identified the friction in traditional map applications where users must manually search, navigate multiple screens, and interact with account-based ecosystems before discovering nearby places.
  • Built a privacy-first local discovery platform that instantly surfaces nearby restaurants, cafés, hotels, parks, attractions, and shopping locations using real-time GPS coordinates.
  • Reduced manual effort compared to conventional map workflows by eliminating repeated searches, category switching, and location entry — users discover their surroundings immediately after opening the application.
  • Implemented automatic location-aware updates and radius-based filtering to keep recommendations relevant as users move through different areas.
  • Designed the system entirely on open geographic data infrastructure using OpenStreetMap, Nominatim, and Overpass APIs — a fully serverless, cost-free architecture.
  • Focused on simplicity and speed by removing accounts, tracking, social features, and unnecessary UI complexity to create a true "open and discover" experience.
Field note

A deliberate exercise in subtraction: no accounts, no tracking, no feed. The entire product is the three seconds between opening the app and seeing what's around you.

No. 04

San-Loc

Tourist Safety System — Smart India Hackathon

Stack — HTML · CSS · JavaScript · Node.js · Express.js · MongoDB · JWT Authentication

  • Designed a tourist safety solution using digital identity verification, geofencing, and rule-based emergency alerts for high-risk zones.
  • Explored how location intelligence and geofencing could proactively identify high-risk areas and trigger contextual safety recommendations.
  • Designed rule-based emergency workflows capable of notifying authorities or emergency contacts during critical situations.
  • Focused on reducing response time during emergencies by enabling automated alerts and location-aware assistance mechanisms.
  • Evaluated the challenges of balancing user privacy, security, and accessibility while designing safety-critical systems.
  • Gained valuable experience translating a real-world public safety problem into a scalable technology solution under hackathon constraints.
Field note

Safety-critical systems invert the usual trade-offs: a false alarm is annoying, but a missed one is unacceptable. Designing under that asymmetry — on a hackathon clock — was the real lesson.

No. 05

MarketMind

AI-Powered Competitive Intelligence Platform

Stack — TypeScript · Next.js · React · Tailwind CSS · Supabase · Prisma · Inngest · OpenRouter

  • Saw a few early-stage startups tracking competitors manually — spreadsheets, bookmarked pricing pages, checking rival sites week after week — and built MarketMind so they can analyse their competitive landscape whenever they want.
  • Designed it as a briefing, not a dashboard: one URL in, and in about 60 seconds the engine reads the product the way an analyst would.
  • Built automatic competitor discovery that maps and ranks the entire competitive landscape from that single URL.
  • Implemented continuous monitoring of thousands of public signals — read around the clock, so the market is watched even when the team isn't.
  • Ensured raw events never reach the user: every signal arrives already thought through, with why it matters and a recommended action attached.
  • Kept facts, inferences, and recommendations strictly distinct — verified sources are labelled as verified, and AI inferences carry their own confidence scores.
Field note

Most tools hand you a dashboard and leave the thinking to you. MarketMind is built to be a briefing — the first thing you open every morning, so you know your market before it moves.
